Overall research skills in HCI and VR

  • Identify research problems (related to human task performance in VR)
  • Propose new user interaction (gestural) techniques to solve research problems
  • Implement the functional prototypes of the new interaction techniques
  • Conduct a full-flow of user study (lab-based controlled experiments with over hundreds subjects)
    • Define hypotheses
    • Identify metrics and Design pre-, in-, post-experiment questionnaires
    • Define experimental tasks
    • Design and Implement the tasks on the server and client sides (e.g., Linux-based CAVE VR system and iOS/Android devices)
    • Analyze the data (performing statistical tests using SPSS and MATLab)
    • Present the results

Spring 2013

PhD Dissertation

Title : Action-Inspired Approach to Design of Navigation Techniques for Effective Spatial Learning in 3-D Virtual Environments

Contributions : Provided integrated insights from multidisciplinary theories to understand human (spatial) learning, Proposed a new action-inspired approach to design of navigation techniques for effective spatial learning, and Demonstrated the effectiveness of the proposed approach using newly developed navigation techniques (based on the action-based approach) through several empirical studies; Originalty, Impact, and Significance related to Action, Perception, and Learning

New Interaction Techniques Developed:

    Finger-driven interaction technique (Finger-Walking in Place: FWIP)
    Sensor fusion based walking in place (SF-WIP) technique

Human Subjects Studies : Usability and Comparative (preliminary, formative and summative) studies with over hundreds human subjects using quantitive and qualitative measurements
