About Me

Before I joined Virginia Tech in 2003, I had some industrial experiences as a Software Engineer in South Korea over 5 years. I defended my Master's thesis at the department of Computer Sicence of Virginia Tech in Winter 2005. I am currently pursuing my Ph.D.degree in the Human Computer Interaction (HCI) area . For my PhD dissertation, my research work is focused on human spatial learning in 3D virtual environments by providing a theory-based interaction design methodology. I am also interested in making new interfaces/interaction techniques combined with emerging technologies, which may give people seamless transition between virtual and real worlds for communication. 

Research Interests

  • Human Computer Interaction, User Interaction in VR, Use of (emerging/existing) technologies to maximaze user experience, Spatial cognition, perception and action, human spatial learning, Touch-based tactile feedback

Research Projects (HCI)

  • The effect of actions performed by different effectors (i.e., body parts) for spatial learning in virtual environments - present (since 2008)
    • Comparative user study: Joystick based technique vs. Tablet-size device (e.g., Lemur)/iPhone/iPod Touch/iPad based FWIP vs. Android platform device (e.g., Motorola Droid A855) based Walking in Place technique (on-going)
  • FWIP(Finger-Walking In Place): Development of finger-driven interaction technique for navigation tasks in virtual environments - present (since 2007).
  • Tangible Information-rich Space: The information space supported by tangible-embodied user interfaces – past (2007).
  • Tangible User Interface for CAVE applications using Augmented Reality technique. This study contributed to the alternative interface without the dedicated tracking input devices to a CAVE system. - past (2005)
  • Mobile Augmented Reality: Visual and aural augmentation with PDA – past (2004).

  • Touchless Interface: It is developed for community interaction in a public space – present (since 2007).
  • DREAM (Discrete REconfigurable Aural Matrix) : This research is to investigate human anterior aural perception. It is a multi-speaker array technology designed to sonify spatial visual data by utilizing each individual speaker as an aural counterpart to a pixel or a pixel cluster of a visual display surface such as TV or an LCD screen – present (since 2007).
    • Implementation (using Max/MSP/Jitter, Java): Controlling of 24 speakers and Wiimote.
      • User study: 1st experiment to portray primitive shapes (point, line, triangle, rectangle and circle) with one sound sample (2007), 2nd experiment to portray scenes with 3-4 complex shapes (circle and polygons) (2009).
    • Implementation (using Max/MSP/Jitter, Java): Controlling of 24 speakers and Pen & Tablen user interaction.
      • User Study: Will be performed in April, 2010.
